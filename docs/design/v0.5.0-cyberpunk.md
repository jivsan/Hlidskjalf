# Hlidskjalf v0.5.0 — cyberpunk pass (full send, not cringe)

Supersedes `v0.3.5-design-system.md`. Everything that document locked in about
**palette, typography, color semantics, and density still holds.** What changes:
the "no new animations" and "when unsure, make it quieter" rules are lifted —
v0.5.0 spends atmosphere on purpose. This document is the budget and the
guardrails. If a choice isn't covered here, the v0.3.5 doc still answers it.

> The one-line brief, verbatim: **"full send, but don't make it cringe."**

## Thesis, restated

Hlidskjalf is the operator's high seat. v0.3.5 built the seat — quiet, precise,
dense. v0.5.0 turns the lights down and the instruments on: the room gets dark
and atmospheric, and the *live* things — status dots, the wordmark, the active
guest, primary actions — pick up a physical neon glow. The effect should read
as **a real operations floor at night**, not a movie hacker desktop.

Machine data in JetBrains Mono (`.metric`), the human interface in Archivo.
That split is still the concept. Glow never touches prose.

## Foundation (already landed)

`tailwind.config.js` is the single source of truth for color and emits every
token as a `--c-<name>` CSS custom property. Canvas consumers (Recharts, xterm,
noVNC) read them through `lib/theme.ts`'s `cssVar()`. **No new hex values may
appear anywhere** — not in TSX, not in CSS, not in chart config. If the palette
ever moves, it moves in exactly one file.

Palette (unchanged): bg `#15161f` · abyss `#0e0e16` · surface `#1e2030` ·
surface-2 `#262a41` · border `#2b2f45` · fg `#c8d3f5` · muted `#727aa3` ·
pink `#ff4fa3` · cyan `#2de2e6` · red `#f7768e` · amber `#e0af68` ·
green `#22c55e`.

Meaning (unchanged): **cyan = live/healthy/primary · pink = seat/selection
ONLY · amber = attention · red = danger.** Do not spray pink.

## The ambient scene

Four layers, all `position: fixed`, `pointer-events: none`, animating
**transform/opacity only** (no layout, no paint storms). Ordered back to front:

1. **Aurora** (the existing body gradients, deepened): cyan 5% → 7%,
   pink 3.5% → 5%. A slow ≥45s positional drift so the room feels aired, not
   static.
2. **Grid horizon**: a faint perspective floor grid — two repeating
   linear-gradients (verticals + horizontals) on a `rotateX` plane, cyan at
   ~5% opacity, drifting forward over ~60s. It must read as *depth at the edge
   of vision*. If you can see it while reading a VM's config, it is too strong.
3. **Scanlines**: `repeating-linear-gradient` at ~3% opacity, `body::after`,
   top z-index. **Static. Never animated.** No rolling CRT bar, ever. They
   should be invisible at arm's length and just barely register up close —
   texture, not effect.
4. **Vignette**: a gentle radial darkening at the corners. Pulls the eye to
   the work.

Everything animated here (aurora drift, grid drift) freezes under
`prefers-reduced-motion` — the scene reduces to its static layers (scanlines
and vignette may stay; they don't move).

## The glow vocabulary

Glow is a **physical property of live things**, not decoration. It is permitted
in exactly four places:

1. **The wordmark** — the sign over the seat. Two-tone (split coloring as
   today), each side glowing its own accent.
2. **Live status dots** — `box-shadow` bloom on the dot itself (cyan when the
   thing is alive). The dot is an LED; LEDs bloom.
3. **The VM status chip** — its ● and its cyan segments only. systemd-style:
   the *running* part glows, the unit name stays flat.
4. **Primary actions on hover/focus** — see shards below: the *silhouette*
   glows, not the label.

Provided as `.glow-cyan` / `.glow-pink` (text-shadow) and dot-level box-shadow
utilities. **Nowhere else.** Never body text, never whole cards, never tables,
never the fleet grid. A page where everything glows is a page where nothing is
alive.

## The animation vocabulary (user-mandated: "animations would be sick")

v0.3.5 banned new animations outright. v0.5.0 has an animation *budget* — and
spends it. Every animation must fit one of three classes:

**Ambient loops** — run forever, but at periods ≥ 45s so they register as
*weather*, not motion. Subliminal by definition.

- `aurora-drift` — the body gradients wander slowly (≥45s).
- `grid-drift` — the perspective floor slides forward (~60s), like the room is
  idling.
- `dot-bloom` — live status dots breathe (box-shadow swelling ~3s; the ONE
  exception to the ≥45s rule, because an LED that doesn't pulse reads as dead).

**Event animations** — fire once per trigger (mount, hover, focus, value
change), then settle. These are where the showmanship lives:

- `power-on` (login only) — the card snaps on like a CRT: a thin bright line
  expands open + a single brightness flash, ~400ms. Once per page load.
- `neon-ignite` (login only) — the wordmark flickers twice as it lights, then
  burns steady. Once per page load, after power-on.
- `shard-sheen` — an energy sweep crosses a sharded button on hover (a
  translated gradient, ~300ms). Re-triggers per hover, never loops idle.
- `nav-slide` — the active nav indicator *slides* to the newly active item
  (transform transition), it does not teleport.
- `card-lift` — interactive cards rise 1px and their hairline brightens on
  hover (transform, 100ms). Physical, like touching a key.
- `chart-draw` — primary chart strokes draw themselves in on mount
  (stroke-dashoffset, ≤600ms) and area fills fade up. Node page keeps its perf:
  draw-in only, no per-poll re-animation.
- `boot-type` — the one typed line (see below).
- `rate-bar` — top-talker bars on the Switch page ease to new widths as rates
  change (transition on transform/scaleX).

**Never** — the cringe list, still banned absolutely:

- Matrix rain, cascading characters, anything that types lies ("hacking…").
- A rolling CRT brightness bar. (Scanlines stay **static** — they are texture,
  not effect. Animating them is the first thing that reads as parody.)
- Fast loops that demand attention: anything under ~2s that isn't a status LED.
- A second typed line. One is style; two is a theme park.

All animation is **transform/opacity/filter only** — no layout thrash, no paint
storms, and everything above dies under `prefers-reduced-motion` (the static
scene — scanlines, vignette, settled gradients — may remain).

## The shape language

**Shards.** Buttons and badges trade their radius for a chamfer — two corner
cuts via `clip-path`, as if stamped out of plate. Implementation rules, learned
the hard way:

- `clip-path` clips **borders and outlines too**. Sharded buttons therefore go
  *borderless*: an accent-tinted fill (`bg-cyan/10`, hover `bg-cyan/20`) with
  accent text.
- Hover and keyboard focus use `filter: drop-shadow(0 0 6px …)`, which follows
  the clipped silhouette — neon hover **and** a focus indicator in one.
  `:focus-visible` must remain obvious; verify per page, don't assume.
- Tables, inputs, and dense list rows **stay rectangular**. Chamfers everywhere
  is a Vegas marquee. Shards mark *actions*; data keeps its grid.

**Corner brackets.** `.card-brackets` draws L-shaped corner ticks (cyan/40) on
a card via pseudo-elements — the targeting-reticle frame. Always-on for the two
signature panels (the VM-detail header, the login card); hover-reveal anywhere
else it appears; never on tables or wells.

**Inputs** keep their radius (they are data fields, not actions) but their
focus state gains a soft cyan drop-shadow alongside the border change.

## Signature surfaces

- **Login** is the hero: the full ambient scene, the wordmark at its largest
  with both glows, the card keeps its pink→cyan hairline and gains always-on
  corner brackets. One — exactly one — JetBrains Mono boot line types itself
  once (see "the one typed line" below).
- **The masthead**: wordmark two-tone glow, the active nav item's left bar
  blooms (box-shadow on the existing `::before`), the node chip's live dot
  pings a halo. The rail itself stays quiet.
- **VM detail header**: status chip glow per the vocabulary; corner brackets
  always on.

Every other page inherits the scene, shards, and focus states from the shared
classes and is touched only where something clashes.

## The one typed line

The login page renders pre-auth and deliberately names no host. Exactly one
line, real copy (`> the high seat — watching every guest`), types itself once
at human speed. The cursor blinks a few times, then retires. Any keypress or
click completes it instantly. `prefers-reduced-motion` renders it complete.
**No fake hacking, no host or node names, no progress bars to nowhere, nothing
that types "mainframe".** If a second typed line ever appears anywhere in the
app, the bit is dead — one is style, two is a theme park.

## The not-cringe contract (hard rules)

1. Glow only on accent-colored **live** things, per the vocabulary above.
2. Scanlines static, ~3%, invisible at arm's length. No CRT bar, no Matrix
   rain, nothing that types lies.
3. Exactly one typed line in the entire app.
4. **No palette change, no new fonts, no new hues.** The cyberpunk comes from
   light and geometry, not from new colors.
5. Animations come only from the vocabulary above: ambient loops stay
   subliminal (≥45s), event animations fire once per trigger and settle.
   `prefers-reduced-motion` kills every one of them.
6. Data density, contrast, keyboard focus, and mobile layout **must not
   regress**. Contrast ratios stay ≥ the v0.3.5 baseline; the scene lives
   behind the content, never on top of it (scanlines excepted — and they sit
   at 3% precisely so they don't count as "on top").
7. The switch faceplate keeps its photoreal metal skin. It is a physical
   object rendered honestly; the neon room is for the panel, not the hardware.

## What "done" looks like

At arm's length the app reads as *the same panel, at night, with the
instruments lit*. Up close: the grid breathes at the horizon, live dots bloom,
sharded actions sheen under the pointer, the nav indicator glides, charts draw
themselves in, and login powers on like a CRT and says its one line. Nothing
loops loudly, nothing begs, nothing types lies. Full send; no cringe.
